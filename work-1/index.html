Written Reflection

For the first work-in-progress submission, I was responsible for building a basic website structure with multiple links, primarily to display my work in this course. I used a solid understanding of HTML elements, coupled with a basic knowledge of CSS, to flesh out my website and create an immersive and straightforward experience for its users. In this essay, I will be describing my approach to this assignment, including my design vision. I will also be discussing the direction I would like to take my work in order to improve it for the next submission.

To begin with, my design vision was initially very simple; I was looking to create a website that internet users would be inclined to use. When I am browsing the internet and come across a website that does not immediately appeal to me, I usually do not waste time on the site. There are often millions of other web pages that contain the same information that I am looking for, so I am usually quick to back out and look for another website that is more appealing to me. Similarly, when people click on the link to my website, my goal is to keep them on my page for as long as possible so that I can convey all of the information that it contains.

I believe this is best accomplished by simplicity. Firstly, the navigation of the website should be clearly labelled and easy to find. If it is not obvious how to find the next pages on the website, then people will not get a chance to see them. Also, if people click on a link expecting to see a certain page, and I show them something different, then their experience becomes negative and they are likely to leave. This is why a clear and convenient navigation bar is a key element to any website and was one of the main focuses to my design concept. I made the decision to place my navigation at the very top of the page so that it is the first thing users see.

Secondly, the text and color scheme play a large role in simplicity. My goal is to convey the information on my website in the form of written text. Therefore, it was important for me to pick text and background colors that compliment each other and make the words on the page easier to read. Similarly, I decided to use calmer colors such as grey and green throughout my site because bright and colorful background colors can be extremely distracting from the important content. I especially disagreed with the use of a background image because they can make a page feel cluttered or distracting as well. Finally, I felt that by keeping my text formatting consistent throughout my website, I was simplifying the website so that my users would know what to expect on every page. By removing any unwelcome surprises, I felt like I was more likely to keep people on my website for longer and provide them with a positive experience.

In order to achieve this simplicity and ease-of-use that I was striving for, I also looked online to currently published websites for guidance. One of my inspirations was Wikipedia, an open source online encyclopedia used by millions. While their site contains much more text and information than mine, I was impressed by its organization and clearness. Each section of text was clearly labelled and accurately described to the reader what they would be reading. There is also an extremely easy to use navigation bar at the top and side of the page. Finally, their plain white background serves its purpose of emphasizing the written text, and their use of pictures was simple and relevant. I believe that all of these things make Wikipedia one of the most frequently used online resources, and I was hoping to replicate their strategy in my approach.

In addition, revisioning was a key component to creating my website. While these components I previously mentioned sound fine, I did not know how effective they were for my purposes until I actually implemented the elements and viewed the site. One of the benefits of using GitHub pages for this assignment was that I was able to frequently commit changes and rebuild the site. For every display and styling element I chose to add, I made sure that they reacted with my content in the way that I intended before continuing. For example, the background of my website was initially dark green. After adding the navigation, I determined that the text color that worked best for the hyperlinks was blue since that is what people are most familiar with. But, after implementing both features, I found that my navigation was very difficult to read, and so I revised my design and change my background color to light grey. This was an important step in creating a successful and immersive website for my users.

Finally, although I am proud of my completed submission, I recognize that there are areas in which I need to improve. To begin with, I found it difficult to grasp the spatial formatting concepts in HTML and CSS. On all of my pages, the information is simply vertical and reads from top to bottom. I attempted to arrange things differently on the page, from left to right, but my formatting frequently messed up the spacing, padding, and margins. In addition, I am looking forward to improving my Photoshopping skills. I am currently self-taught, but I know I can benefit greatly from a structured lesson. These photo editing concepts can also be applied to my web design, since ideas such as color, white space, and perspective are extremely important in both areas.

In conclusion, I believe the simplicity and attention to detail my website boasts make it successful and immersive for the user, and while I am proud of it, it is a work-in-progress that I look forward to improving.
